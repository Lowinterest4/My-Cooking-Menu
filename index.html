Appetizer ‚Äì Lumpiang Shanghai

Ingredients:

  • Ground pork
  • Minced vegetables
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt & pepper
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil

Process:

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground pork, minced vegetables, garlic, soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix thoroughly until well combined.
  2. Place a small amount of the filling on the wrapper. Roll tightly, folding the sides inward.
  3. Seal the edge with a bit of water to prevent the roll from opening during frying.
  4. Heat enough oil in a deep pan over medium heat.
  5. Fry the lumpia in batches until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  6. Drain on paper towels and serve with sweet chili sauce.

Appetizer ‚Äì Ukoy

Ingredients:

  • Small shrimps
  • Grated papaya or squash
  • Flour
  • Cornstarch
  • Egg
  • Salt & pepper
  • Water
  • Cooking oil

Process:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine shrimps, grated papaya or squash, flour, and cornstarch.
  2. Add the egg, followed by water, salt, and pepper. Mix until a thick batter forms.
  3. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  4. Scoop a ladleful of batter and gently pour into the hot oil.
  5. Flatten slightly with a spoon and fry until edges turn golden.
  6. Flip to cook the other side evenly until crisp.
  7. Serve hot with spiced vinegar.

  • Carrots
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt & pepper
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • Cooking oil

Process:

  1. Heat oil in a pan and saut√© garlic and onion until aromatic.
  2. Add carrots and cook for 2‚Äì3 minutes.
  3. Add bean sprouts and stir gently to avoid breaking them.
  4. Season with soy sauce, salt, and pepper. Mix evenly.
  5. Remove from heat and let the filling cool completely.
  6. Place filling on wrappers and roll tightly.
  7. Deep fry until crispy and golden brown.
  8. Serve with vinegar dipping sauce.

Main Course ‚Äì Adobong Manok

Ingredients:

  • Chicken pieces
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Garlic
  • Bay leaves
  • Pepper
  • Water

Process:

  1. Place chicken in a bowl and add soy sauce, crushed garlic, pepper, and bay leaves. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat a bit of oil in a pot. Add garlic and saut√© until light brown.
  3. Add chicken (without the marinade first) and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Pour in the marinade and add water as needed to cover the chicken.
  5. Simmer uncovered for 10 minutes then pour vinegar. Do not stir until it boils.
  6. Lower heat and simmer for 30‚Äì40 minutes until chicken is tender.
  7. Optional: Let the sauce reduce for a thicker, richer adobo.

Main Course ‚Äì Sinigang

Ingredients:

  • Pork belly or ribs
  • Tamarind mix
  • Tomatoes
  • Onion
  • Radish
  • Sitaw
  • Kangkong
  • Green chili
  • Salt & pepper

Process:

  1. Place pork in a pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil.
  2. Skim off scum that rises to the top for a clear broth.
  3. Add tomatoes and onion, then simmer for 40‚Äì60 minutes until pork is tender.
  4. Add radish and cook for 5 minutes.
  5. Add sitaw and green chili.
  6. Stir in tamarind mix and simmer for another 3‚Äì5 minutes.
  7. Add kangkong leaves last and turn off heat.
  8. Season with salt and pepper before serving.

  7. Add papaya or sayote and cook until tender.

  9. Serve hot.

  • Cabbage
  • Carrots
  • Onion
  • Peppercorn
  • Water
  • Salt & pepper

Process:

  3. Add onion and peppercorn, then simmer for 1.5‚Äì2 hours until beef is tender.
  4. Add potatoes and carrots. Cook for 10 minutes.

  7. Serve warm.

Dessert ‚Äì Fruit Salad

Ingredients:

  • Mixed fruits
  • Condensed milk
  • All-purpose cream

Process:

  1. Drain canned fruits or prepare fresh fruits by cutting them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. In a large bowl, combine fruits, condensed milk, and all-purpose cream.
  3. Mix gently to avoid crushing soft fruits.
  4. Chill for at least 2 hours before serving.

Dessert ‚Äì Halo-Halo

Ingredients:

  • Crushed ice
  • Evaporated milk
  • Sweetened beans
  • Nata de coco
  • Leche flan
  • Ube halaya
  • Bananas
  • Jelly

Process:

  1. Prepare a tall glass and add sweetened beans, nata de coco, jelly, and sliced bananas at the bottom.
  2. Add a generous amount of crushed ice on top of the ingredients.
  3. Pour evaporated milk slowly over the ice.
  4. Add a spoonful of ube halaya and a slice of leche flan on top.
  5. Serve and mix before eating.

Dessert ‚Äì Bananacue

Ingredients:

  • Saba bananas
  • Brown sugar
  • Cooking oil
  • Bamboo sticks

Process:

  1. Heat a generous amount of oil in a pan over medium heat.
  2. Add bananas and fry for 2 minutes.
  3. Sprinkle brown sugar directly into the oil.
  4. Allow sugar to melt and coat the bananas evenly.
  5. Continue cooking until bananas are caramelized.
  6. Remove from oil and skewer onto bamboo sticks.
  7. Cool slightly before serving.

Dessert ‚Äì Leche Flan

Ingredients:

  • Egg yolks
  • Condensed milk
  • Evaporated milk
  • Vanilla
  • Sugar (for caramel)

Process:

  1. Heat sugar in your llanera mold over low heat until melted and golden.
  2. Spread the caramel evenly and allow it to cool and harden.
  3. In a bowl, whisk egg yolks gently to avoid bubbles.
  4. Add condensed milk and evaporated milk, then mix until smooth.
  5. Add vanilla extract and strain mixture for a smooth texture.
  6. Pour the mixture into the caramel-lined mold.
  7. Cover with foil and steam for 30‚Äì40 minutes.
  8. Cool completely before removing from mold.
